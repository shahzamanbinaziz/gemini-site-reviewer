Area for Immediate Improvement: Ads.txt


Problem: The `ads.txt` file is showing an "⚠️ Error checking" status. This is a critical issue for ad monetization and transparency.



  • Why it Matters:

    • `ads.txt` (Authorized Digital Sellers) helps prevent ad fraud by allowing buyers to verify that sellers (publishers) are legitimate.

    • It ensures you receive payments from authorized ad partners and builds trust with advertisers and ad exchanges.

    • A non-functional or missing `ads.txt` can lead to lost ad revenue, reduced ad fill rates, and potential removal from ad networks.



  • How to Fix:

    • Create/Correct `ads.txt` File: Ensure a valid `ads.txt` file exists at the root of your domain (e.g., `https://example.com/ads.txt`). It should be a plain text file.

    • Populate with Authorized Sellers: Obtain the correct lines from each of your advertising partners (e.g., Google AdSense, other ad networks, SSPs). Each line typically includes:

      • The domain name of the ad exchange/company.

      • Your Publisher ID (account ID) with that ad exchange.

      • The relationship type (DIRECT if you directly own the account, RESELLER if you use a third-party to manage it).

      • (Optional) A certification authority ID.


      Example: google.com, pub-0000000000000000, DIRECT, f08c47fec0942fa0

    • Verify Accessibility: After uploading the corrected file, check if it's publicly accessible and free from errors by navigating to its URL in a browser. Ensure there are no server errors (like 404 Not Found) and that the content is correctly displayed.
